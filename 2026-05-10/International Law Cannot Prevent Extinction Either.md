### Main Topic: AI Risk and Law

**Main Thesis:** International laws are ineffective at stopping an AI extinction event because powerful nations ignore them, and there is no shared agreement on the actual risk.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that international laws cannot prevent the extinction caused by advanced AI because powerful countries often ignore these rules without consequence. Furthermore, the race for AI is driven by different motivations than the nuclear race, and because there is no global agreement on how dangerous AI is, no one is motivated to act to prevent disaster.

**The Bottom Line:** > Since there is no global consensus on the danger of AI, the perceived payoff for ignoring the risk remains too high for any country to follow international rules.
