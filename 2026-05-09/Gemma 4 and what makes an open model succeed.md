### Main Topic: Open AI Models
**Main Thesis:** Open AI models are gaining popularity, but their success is harder to achieve than closed models due to challenges in benchmarking and the complexity of their tooling at release.
**Simple Summary:** The article discusses the rise of open AI models like Qwen and Gemma, but points out that they aren’t always as good as the closed models (like ChatGPT) when they’re first released. It’s tricky to tell how good they really are because the initial benchmarks aren’t very helpful. Plus, the tools that people use to work with these models often aren’t fully ready yet, making it harder to use them effectively. 

**The Bottom Line:** > Don’t just look at a model’s score on a test; consider how easy it is to actually *use* and adapt the model for your needs – that’s the key to understanding its true potential.
