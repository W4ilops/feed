### Main Topic: Modern Terminal Experiences
**Main Thesis:** The author describes what makes a “good” modern terminal experience for them and suggests simple ways to achieve it with minimal configuration.

**Simple Summary:** The author wants a terminal that’s easy to use and doesn’t require a ton of complicated setup. They value features like being able to copy and paste commands, remembering commands you’ve used, having a colorful display, and seamless integration with other programs. To get this, they use the “fish” shell, a terminal emulator with 24-bit colors, and the Neovim text editor, all with just a little bit of basic setup.

**The Bottom Line:** > If you want a comfortable terminal experience, you don’t need to be a tech expert – just choose a few key tools and set up a few simple things to make it work for you.
