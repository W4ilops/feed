### Main Topic: WebRTC Audio Issues
**Main Thesis:** The author argues that WebRTC’s focus on speed leads to audio quality degradation, which is undesirable for users seeking accurate AI responses.
**Simple Summary:** WebRTC is a technology used for real-time communication, like video calls. It works by sending audio quickly, even if it means the audio gets a little messed up. The author believes this is bad because users want the AI to understand them perfectly, and waiting a little longer for clear audio would be better, but WebRTC is designed to always be fast.
**The Bottom Line:** > WebRTC prioritizes speed over audio quality, creating a frustrating experience for users who need reliable AI interactions. It’s a trade-off that limits the usefulness of AI voice applications.
