### Main Topic: Outcome Equality
**Main Thesis:** The article argues that laws requiring governments to fix differences in results (like income or employment) are costly, ineffective, and likely to cause more problems than they solve.
**Simple Summary:** Many countries, including the UK, are considering laws that force organizations to try and make everyone have the same results – like equal pay or job opportunities – based on things like race or gender. However, the author says these laws don’t really work, just create a lot of extra paperwork and cost money, without actually changing people’s lives. They suggest more money and training won’t fix the problem.
**The Bottom Line:** These attempts to force equal outcomes are a waste of money and time, and won’t actually solve inequality problems.
