### Main Topic: AI in Driving
**Main Thesis:** The article proposes that Large Language Models (LLMs) could be the unexpected solution to the challenges of creating truly autonomous self-driving cars.
**Simple Summary:** Back in the day, self-driving cars were built with lots of separate computer programs, each doing one small job. But now, a new idea called “End-to-End learning” is gaining traction, where one big AI system handles everything. The article explains how Large Language Models (LLMs), like the ones powering chatbots, might be the next big step, offering a simpler and potentially more effective way to make cars drive themselves. It starts by explaining what LLMs are – basically, how they turn words into numbers so a computer can understand them.
**The Bottom Line:** >  Traditional approaches to self-driving cars haven’t fully solved the problem, and LLMs might offer a simpler, more powerful way to achieve true autonomy, even if it’s a bit of a mystery how they’ll do it.
