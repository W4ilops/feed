### Main Topic: Question Answering
**Main Thesis:** The article explains how to build AI systems that can answer factual questions without needing specific context, focusing on "open-book" question answering.
**Simple Summary:** This article describes how to create AI programs that can answer questions about facts, like a really smart chatbot. These programs don't need to have all the answers memorized; instead, they can search for information online (like using Wikipedia) to find the right answer. The article also warns that simply training AI on existing question-and-answer sets can lead to problems if the questions are too similar, and suggests a method called "retriever-reader" that uses a knowledge base to find relevant information.
**The Bottom Line:** > AI systems that can answer questions without needing a lot of pre-existing knowledge are becoming more common, but it’s important to make sure the questions they’re asked aren’t too similar to questions they’ve already seen, or the AI might not work correctly.
