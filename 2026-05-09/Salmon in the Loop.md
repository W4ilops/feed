### Main Topic: Fish Counting
**Main Thesis:** The article explains how hydroelectric dams must track fish populations to comply with environmental regulations and demonstrate they aren’t harming the fish.
**Simple Summary:** Big dams need to count the fish that swim past them to make sure they aren’t hurting the fish populations. Dams block fish from moving around, which can be bad for the fish. To show they’re not causing problems, dam operators collect data on how many fish pass through, often using trained observers or automated systems. This data is important because the government wants to protect endangered fish species.
**The Bottom Line:** > Dams need to carefully monitor fish populations to avoid breaking environmental rules and ensure the survival of threatened fish species.
