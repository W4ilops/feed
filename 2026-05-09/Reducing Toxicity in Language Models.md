### Main Topic: Language Model Toxicity
**Main Thesis:** The article discusses the challenges of identifying and controlling “toxic” behavior in large language models, which are prone to learning harmful biases from online data.
**Simple Summary:** Big computer programs that can write and talk like people are learning bad things from the internet – like being rude or hateful. It’s hard to know exactly what “bad” means, and even harder to make sure these programs don’t say or do harmful things. The article explains how experts, regular people, and trained moderators can help label examples of toxic language, but it’s a complicated process.
**The Bottom Line:** > It’s crucial to carefully manage how these powerful language models are trained to avoid them repeating and amplifying harmful biases and offensive content in the real world.
