### Main Topic: Learning How to Act

**Main Thesis:** This article explains “policy gradient” – a way for computers to learn how to make decisions in situations where they’re trying to get rewards, like a game.

**Simple Summary:**  The article is about a technique called “policy gradient” used in computer programs that learn to make choices. It’s like teaching a robot to play a game by rewarding good moves and letting it figure out the best way to win. It uses math to describe how the robot learns and what’s important to consider, like how likely it is to get rewards in the future.

**The Bottom Line:** >  Policy gradient helps computers learn to make smart decisions by directly figuring out which actions lead to the most rewards, making them better at tasks like playing games or controlling robots.
