### Main Topic: Future Proofing Software
**Main Thesis:** The author argues that attempting to “future proof” software – designing it to handle every possible future need – is usually a bad idea and often leads to less flexible and more complex systems.
**Simple Summary:**  Programmers often talk about “future proofing” their code, meaning making it work for any possible future use. But the article says this rarely works. It’s better to focus on solving the current problem simply and avoid adding extra features or complexity just in case you might need them later. Overthinking how the code might be used in the future can actually make it harder to maintain and change.
**The Bottom Line:** > Don’t try to predict the future when building software; focus on doing a good job with the current needs, as over-engineering leads to unnecessary complexity and potential problems.
