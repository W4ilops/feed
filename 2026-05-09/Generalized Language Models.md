### Main Topic: Contextual Word Embeddings
**Main Thesis:** The article explains how researchers created word representations that understand the meaning of words based on the sentences they’re in, improving how computers understand language.
**Simple Summary:**  For a long time, computers just looked at words individually, like “apple” always meant the same thing. But scientists figured out a way to make word representations “smart” by looking at the whole sentence. They did this by using a special type of computer model called NMT (Neural Machine Translation) and a technique called CoVe, which learns how words change meaning depending on the context.  It’s like teaching a computer to understand that “I am eating an apple” is different from “I have an Apple phone.”
**The Bottom Line:** > Using context-aware word representations helps computers understand language better, leading to improvements in tasks like answering questions and classifying text.
