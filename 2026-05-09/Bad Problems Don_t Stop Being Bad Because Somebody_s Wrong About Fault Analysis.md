### Main Topic: Explanation vs. Accountability
**Main Thesis:** The article argues that people often try to deflect blame by explaining *why* something happened, rather than addressing the underlying problem or taking responsibility.

**Simple Summary:** People sometimes try to make a situation seem okay by giving a long explanation of why something happened, instead of actually fixing the problem or admitting fault. This is a mistake because explaining the reasons doesn't solve the issue. The author uses examples of a misleading news headline, a safety problem at an AI company, and a cruise ship outbreak to illustrate this tendency.

**The Bottom Line:** > Don’t get distracted by *why* something happened; focus on what needs to be fixed and who needs to take action.
