### Main Topic: AI Model Progress
**Main Thesis:** The hype around open AI models catching up to closed models is overblown; the gap remains relatively stable, and public benchmarks don't accurately reflect the true state of AI progress.

**Simple Summary:** This article explains that while new open AI models are constantly appearing, they aren’t actually getting much better than closed models. Experts believe the gap between them is still about 6 months. Public benchmarks used to measure AI performance are flawed and can be easily manipulated, so they don’t give a true picture of how quickly AI is improving. Open models are likely to stay competitive in coding, but overall, the leading labs in the US still have a significant advantage.

**The Bottom Line:** > Don’t be fooled by the constant buzz about open AI models; the reality is that closed models still hold a performance edge, and relying on simple benchmarks can be misleading.
