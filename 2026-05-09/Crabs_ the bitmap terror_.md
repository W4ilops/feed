### Main Topic: Computer Screens
**Main Thesis:** The article describes a simple, early computer program called "Crabs" that demonstrated a clever way to interact with bitmap screens by "erasing" applications beneath windows.
**Simple Summary:** Back when computer screens were made of pixels (like old computer monitors), programmers found ways to make them more interesting. This article talks about a program called "Crabs" that moved across the screen and erased the programs underneath it, like a little crab walking on a digital map. It shows how programmers experimented with ways to interact with these early screens.
**The Bottom Line:** > Early computer programmers were creative and found fun ways to play with the limitations of bitmap screens, leading to programs like "Crabs" that demonstrated unique interactions with the user interface.
