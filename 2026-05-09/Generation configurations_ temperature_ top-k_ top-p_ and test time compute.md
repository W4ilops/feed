### Main Topic: AI Probabilities
**Main Thesis:** AI models don't give definite answers; they provide probabilities, which can lead to inconsistency and errors, especially in factual situations.
**Simple Summary:** AI models aren't always right because they guess based on chance. They give a "best guess" percentage for each answer. This is good for creative tasks, but bad when you need a solid, factual answer. The article explains how these models make guesses (called "sampling") and how to control those guesses to make them more creative or more reliable.
**The Bottom Line:** > AI’s probabilistic nature means it’s not a perfect source of truth, and users need to understand this uncertainty to use it effectively.
