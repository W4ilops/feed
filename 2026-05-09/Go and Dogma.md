### Main Topic: Language Design
**Main Thesis:** The article argues against rigid, unquestioning adherence to design choices in programming languages, emphasizing the importance of explaining both the reasons *for* a decision and the reasons *why* a different approach might have been considered.
**Simple Summary:** When creating programming languages, designers need to be open about why they made certain choices. It’s not enough to just say “this is how we do it.” They should also explain why other options were considered and why they were rejected. This helps users understand the reasoning behind the language and avoids arguments based on simply saying something “must” be done a certain way.
**The Bottom Line:** > Don’t treat language design decisions as absolute rules. Always explain the thought process behind choices, including why alternatives were explored, to foster understanding and avoid unnecessary debates.
