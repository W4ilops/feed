### Main Topic: Community roles

**Main Thesis:** Using the term "organizer" creates false expectations that a community must rely on a single person, which makes the group unstable and creates unnecessary power imbalances.

**Simple Summary:** The author argues that labeling someone as a "community organizer" creates problems. This label suggests that a community *must* have one person in charge, which leads to groups becoming overly dependent on that single person. This reliance makes it hard to train replacements, and it creates an air of importance that makes the organizer feel indispensable.

**The Bottom Line:** > To make community groups stronger and more stable, they should avoid relying on a single organizer and instead rotate the responsibility for running events among all members.
