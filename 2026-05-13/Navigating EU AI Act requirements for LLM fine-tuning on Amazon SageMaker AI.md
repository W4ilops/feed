### Main Topic: AI Regulation Tracking

**Main Thesis:** To comply with the EU AI Act when fine-tuning large language models, organizations must track the computational resources used during training, measured in floating-point operations (FLOPs).

**Simple Summary:** The EU AI Act requires companies to track how much computing power is used when they fine-tune large language models (LLMs). If the fine-tuning uses a large amount of computing power compared to the original model's training, it might trigger new legal responsibilities for the company. Tools like Amazon SageMaker and the Fine-Tuning FLOPs Meter help track these computing costs and calculate whether the fine-tuning crosses the regulatory line.

**The Bottom Line:** > Tracking the computing power used during LLM fine-tuning is essential because it determines whether the work triggers new legal obligations under the EU AI Act.
