### Main Topic: Utopian Socialism

**Main Thesis:** Thinkers like John Stuart Mill used socialist and utopian ideas to justify social change, often overlooking practical reality and rational thought.

**Simple Summary:** The article discusses how some liberals, like John Stuart Mill, promoted the idea of a perfect, ideal society (utopia) to encourage people to work for the common good. Critics, like Ludwig von Mises, argued that this focus on perfect dreams ignores how human nature actually works. The text suggests that these utopian ideas were used by some intellectuals to push for social change, sometimes overriding real-world concerns.

**The Bottom Line:** > While the dream of a perfect society is appealing, it is important to study these ideas carefully because focusing only on ideals can lead to ignoring practical reality.
