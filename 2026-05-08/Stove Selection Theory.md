### Main Topic: Altruism and Evolution
**Main Thesis:** The article argues that the theory of kin selection, which explains altruistic behavior as driven by gene survival, is fundamentally flawed and shouldn’t be used to support objective ethics.

**Simple Summary:** This article discusses a philosopher’s argument that the idea that we’re naturally inclined to help our relatives because it helps our genes survive is actually wrong. The author explains that if genes are what matter, then we should see more selfless behavior from children towards parents than parents show towards children, but we don’t. This creates a big problem for the theory.

**The Bottom Line:** > Evolutionary explanations for morality shouldn't be used to claim that moral truths are real because they simply reflect what helps our genes survive – a theory that doesn’t hold up to close examination.
