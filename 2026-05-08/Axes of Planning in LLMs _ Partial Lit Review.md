### Main Topic: Language Model Planning
**Main Thesis:** The article explores different ways to understand whether language models are “planning,” recognizing that “planning” itself is a flexible concept with various interpretations.

**Simple Summary:** This article examines whether language models actually “plan” when they generate text. It argues that “planning” isn’t one thing, but can be broken down into different categories like how far ahead the model thinks, how detailed its ideas are, and whether it’s considering many choices or just a few. The author uses examples of how models behave – like noticing irrelevant facts, signaling the end of a section, or following a vague outline – to illustrate these different types of planning.

**The Bottom Line:** > It’s important to realize that language models don’t necessarily “plan” in the same way humans do. Instead, they exhibit different behaviors that can be categorized to understand how they approach generating text, and this understanding is crucial for evaluating and improving their capabilities.
