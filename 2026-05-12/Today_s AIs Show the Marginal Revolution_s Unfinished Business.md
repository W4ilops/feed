### Main Topic: Economic History

**Main Thesis:** The way economists chose to measure value—through subjective thought versus mathematical data—created a conflict that ultimately led to the decline of the discipline.

**Simple Summary:** Early economic ideas focused on the idea that value is based on what people choose due to scarcity. Later, economists shifted to focusing heavily on numbers and statistics. This focus on measurement, especially the approach taken by Jevons, led the field to chase data and mathematical proof, which the author argues ultimately caused the discipline to undermine itself.

**The Bottom Line:** > The method used to study economics matters as much as the results, and focusing too much on measurement can distract from the real human actions that shape the economy.
