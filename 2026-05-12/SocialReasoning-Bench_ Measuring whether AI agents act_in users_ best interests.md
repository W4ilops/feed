### Main Topic: AI Social Reasoning

**Main Thesis:** AI agents need social reasoning skills to effectively act on behalf of users in real-world interactions, and a new benchmark is needed to measure this ability.

**Simple Summary:** AI agents that handle tasks need to understand social situations—like negotiating or coordinating schedules—not just completing tasks. The article argues that current AI models often fail in these social contexts because they don't understand what others want or how to advocate for the user. To fix this, researchers created a test called SocialReasoning-Bench to measure whether agents can successfully negotiate and coordinate with others.

**The Bottom Line:** > For AI agents to be truly useful delegates, they must learn how to reason socially and negotiate effectively, similar to how human professionals are expected to act.
