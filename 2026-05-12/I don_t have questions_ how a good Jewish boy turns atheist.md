### Main Topic: Religious Doubt

**Main Thesis:** People often leave religious traditions like Orthodox Judaism when they encounter intellectual problems, especially if they lack the deep, personal spiritual experience that some religious adherents possess.

**Simple Summary:** The author discusses why some people leave Orthodox Judaism. They argue that while many people have questions about religion, those who leave often do so because they find the religious teachings don't align with their personal experience or logic. The author suggests that the deep spiritual connection to God is real for some, but intellectual doubt can break down the religious framework for others.

**The Bottom Line:** > Whether you stay in a religion depends on whether you have a personal, felt experience of that faith, not just intellectual agreement.
